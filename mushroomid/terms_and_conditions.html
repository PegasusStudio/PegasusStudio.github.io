3. Critical Mushroom Safety Warning

Never eat, taste, prepare, sell, serve, handle, or forage a wild mushroom based only on Mushroom ID or any photo-identification app. A wrong identification can cause severe poisoning, permanent injury, organ failure, or death.

The app's suggestions, confidence values, edibility descriptions, toxicity information, warnings, and similar-species information are educational only. They are not proof of identity or safety and are not a substitute for in-person examination by a qualified local mycologist or other appropriate expert.

Cooking, drying, freezing, soaking, or peeling does not make every poisonous mushroom safe. Children and pets should be kept away from unidentified mushrooms.

If mushroom exposure or poisoning may have occurred, contact your local emergency service or poison control center immediately. Do not wait for symptoms and do not rely on this app for diagnosis or treatment.

4. Identification Limitations

Mushrooms can change appearance with age, weather, lighting, damage, camera angle, and growing conditions. Dangerous species may closely resemble edible species. A photo may not show essential features such as the cap surface, underside, stem base, ring, volva, bruising, odor, spore print, habitat, substrate, or microscopic characteristics.

Identification suggestions may be incomplete, outdated, inaccurate, or unavailable. A high confidence value does not establish that a result is correct and does not indicate that a mushroom is safe to eat or touch.
